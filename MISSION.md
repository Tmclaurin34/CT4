## The mission, in one sentence

**Help local businesses keep the customers they already earned — automatically, warmly, and before they're gone for good.**

---

## The problem we exist to solve

A local business loses 20–30% of its customers every year. Almost none of them complain. They don't get angry, they don't find somewhere better — **they just get busy and forget.** A missed appointment becomes a missed month becomes a new habit somewhere else.

The owner is the only person who would notice a regular going quiet — and the owner is the busiest person in the building. So nobody notices. Nobody reaches out. And the marketing industry's answer is to sell the owner *more ads to find strangers*, which costs several times more than keeping the customer they already had.

Clicktide exists because **the cheapest, warmest, most profitable customer any business can win is the one they already won once.**

## What Clicktide actually does (plain words)

1. **Connects to the business's register** — read-only, through the platform's official connection. We can see visits and sales history; we cannot move money or modify their account. One click to disconnect.
2. **Learns every customer's rhythm.** Every customer has a pace — weekly gym visits, a haircut every five weeks. Clicktide learns each one's normal.
3. **Notices when a regular breaks their rhythm** — and starts the Win-Back Playbook:
   - **Day 14:** a friendly check-in under the business's name — by text if that customer has texting consent on file, otherwise by email. No links, no pressure. *"Hey Sarah, it's June's Beauty Bar — been a couple weeks since we saw you! Everything okay?"*
   - **Day 30:** a real printed postcard in their mailbox (about $1.50, addressed and mailed automatically, paid from the owner's wallet).
   - **Day 45:** a small branded gift on their doorstep — a mug, a bottle, with the business's logo. Gifts only ever send with the owner's standing permission.
   - **The moment the customer visits again, all future sends stop automatically.** (One honest caveat: physical mail can't be recalled — a postcard printed before the visit may still arrive. Everything *after* the visit stops.)
4. **Every Monday: the Drift Report** — an email telling the owner who started drifting last week, how long they've been gone, and an estimate of what they're worth.
5. **After visits, customers can be asked how it went** — feedback reaches the owner, and leaving a Google review is made easy. (See internal notes before answering detailed review-policy questions.)

The name is the idea: ads are waves — loud, expensive, they crash and disappear. **Loyalty is the tide. It brings people back.**

## What we believe (answer every question from these)

1. **Customers don't leave — they forget.** Win-backs aren't about winning an argument; they're about being remembered first.
2. **A discount is a transaction. A gift is a relationship.** A coupon says "come spend money." A gift says "you mattered." People don't come back for transactions.
3. **The owner's name is on every message — so never annoy their customers.** Three win-back touches max, then we stop for good. Texting requires consent on file, and STOP is honored automatically. Any visit stops all future sends. Approve-before-send mode means nothing goes out unseen. **Money needs a yes:** Clicktide never spends from the owner's gift wallet without standing permission they explicitly gave.
4. **Retention before acquisition.** The honest, sourced numbers — quote them carefully, as written:
   - Winning a new customer typically costs **5 to 25 times more** than keeping an existing one, depending on the industry *(Harvard Business Review)*
   - Keeping just 5% more customers has been shown to lift profits **25–95%** *(Bain & Company)*
   - The probability of selling to an existing customer is **60–70%**, versus 5–20% for a new prospect *(Marketing Metrics)*
   - Repeat customers spend on average about **67% more** than new ones *(BIA Advisory Services)*
5. **Honest, always.** No fake testimonials, no invented results, sample data is always labeled "sample." We're a new company and we're straightforward about it — being early means founding customers get the founder's personal attention. Never claim a result we can't show; say "usually" and "typically," never "guaranteed."
6. **Small-business first.** Whole-dollar prices, cancel anytime, the customer data belongs to the business — never sold, deleted on request.

## The goal

- **Right now:** our first 100 local businesses, treated so well they tell other owners. Find the vertical that loves us most (salons, gyms, auto shops are the early bets) and go deep.
- **The long game:** become the default retention layer for local commerce — every neighborhood business runs its win-backs on Clicktide the way they run payments on Square.

## Who we serve

Owner-operated local businesses with roughly 200–5,000 customers: salons, barbershops, nail studios, med spas, gyms and yoga studios, auto shops, cleaning services, cafés, restaurants, pet groomers, boutiques. If their customers are *supposed* to come back and sometimes don't, they're ours.

## Questions you will get — and how we answer them

**"Why would I send a gift to someone who hasn't been back? Why reward someone for leaving?"**
> "You're not rewarding the leaving — you're interrupting the forgetting. She didn't choose to leave; life got busy. A discount competes on price, but a gift competes on feeling: it says *you were part of this place and we noticed you were gone.* And it's never the first touch — a friendly check-in goes at day 14, a postcard at day 30. The gift only goes out at day 45, when the relationship is genuinely about to die — a small gift protecting a regular worth hundreds or thousands a year is usually the cheapest save in business. Plus, nobody throws away a free mug: your logo lives on her counter, working, even before she comes back."

**"Won't this spam or annoy my customers?"**
> "Your name is on every message, so the guardrails aren't optional: three win-back touches max, then it stops for good. A return visit stops all future sends. Texts only go to customers with texting consent on file, and STOP is honored automatically. And there's an approve-before-send mode where you read every message before it goes. The messages themselves are warm check-ins, not promotions — no links, no 'DEALS', just 'everything okay? your spot's here.'"

**"So you're tracking my customers? Isn't that creepy?"**
> "It's the same visit history that's already sitting in your register — we just read it and notice patterns, the way you would if you had time. Nothing is collected that you don't already have, nothing is sold, and what comes out the other end isn't surveillance — it's you saying 'we missed you' to someone who used to come in every other week."

**"What can Clicktide see in my register? Is this safe?"**
> "Read-only access through the platform's official connection. We can see visit and sales history so we can spot drifting; we can't move money or change your account, and you can disconnect with one click. Your data is never sold and deleted on request."

**"What does it cost? Any hidden fees?"**
> "Three plans — $29, $99, $299 a month — and texts, emails, and promo-code delivery are included. Physical things cost what they cost, from a pay-per-send wallet you control: about $1.50 a postcard, gifts from a few dollars depending on what you pick (a logo mug runs around $8–20 with shipping). You only pay when something physically sends, and nothing physical sends without your standing permission. No contracts, no setup fees, cancel anytime."

**"What happens after the 30-day free trial?"**
> "Nothing is charged for 30 days, and if you cancel during the trial there's no charge at all. If you stay, your plan starts. Either way the customer data is yours — we delete it on request."

**"Why not just run ads or discounts instead?"**
> "Ads rent attention from strangers, and the odds of selling to a brand-new prospect are 5–20%. Your existing customer buys 60–70% of the time and repeat customers spend about 67% more. Do both if you want — but protect what you already paid to earn first. That's the inexpensive, high-odds money."

**"How is this different from Mailchimp or email blasts?"**
> "Email tools broadcast to everybody and hope. Clicktide watches your register and acts on one person at the right moment — the day *Sarah* breaks her rhythm, Sarah gets a personal check-in citing her real last visit. And it doesn't stop at email: real texts, real postcards, real gifts in the mail — then it goes quiet the moment she returns. It's a win-back engine, not a newsletter."

**"What if the customer comes back on their own?"**
> "Then the clock resets and all future sends stop automatically — we don't message active customers. The one physical exception: if a postcard was already printed and in the mail before their visit, it may still arrive, because paper can't be recalled. Everything after the visit stops."

**"Where do you get my customers' mailing addresses for postcards and gifts?"**
> "Three ways, in order: if your register has the address on file, we import it. If not, the customer gets a friendly note — 'June's Beauty Bar has a little something they want to mail you, tell us where to send it' — with a secure form. And anything still missing, you can fill in yourself when you approve the send. We never buy addresses from data brokers."

**"Do you sell my customer data?"**
> "Never. The data belongs to the business. We use it to notice drift and send the win-backs you approved — that's it — and we delete it on request. (Printing a postcard or shipping a gift does require sharing that recipient's name and address with the print/fulfillment partner — same as any mail service.)"

**"How do the Google reviews work?"**
> "After a visit, customers can be asked how it went. Feedback comes to you either way, and happy customers get a one-tap link to share it on Google. It turns the goodwill you've already earned into reviews, and gives you an early warning when something went wrong." *(If they push on Google's review-policy details, don't improvise — "let me have Charles walk you through exactly how the settings work.")*

**"Is this AI writing to my customers?"**
> "The messages are pre-written templates — you can read and edit every word, and in approve-before-send mode nothing goes out without you. It sounds like you because you control the words."

**"Can you promise me X customers back in a month?"**
> "Honest answer: no — and be careful with anyone who does promise that. What I can show you free is your real number: connect your register read-only and the drift assessment shows you how many customers have drifted and an estimate of what they're worth. Then the 30-day trial lets you watch the win-backs run before you've paid anything. Your own register will make the case better than my promises could."

**"Can I reward my big spenders — someone who just bought a premium package?"**
> "Yes — that's the other half of the tide. Win-backs protect the relationship when it fades; high-ticket thank-yous lock it in right after their biggest yes. Create a campaign with the 'Spent $500+' or 'Spent $1,000+' trigger (any dollar amount works) and pick a premium gift — when a customer's total spending crosses that line, the thank-you fires once, automatically. The moment someone makes their biggest purchase is exactly when a thank-you means the most: it turns buyer's remorse into 'I chose the right place,' and it's the difference between a customer who bought a package and a customer who tells their friends about it."

**"Who's behind this?"**
> "Clicktide is founded and run by Charles — a service of Six Seasons Partners LLC, reachable at support@goclicktide.com (we reply within a business day). Founding customers work directly with the founder; that's the advantage of being early."

**"Can I see it before I pay?"**
> "Yes — the free drift assessment. Connect your register read-only and you'll quickly see how many of your customers have drifted and what they're estimated to be worth. No card needed to look. That number decides for most people."
